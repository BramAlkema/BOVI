B Balanced — Keeping Score

Fiske Mode: Equality Matching — fairness as symmetry, everyone gets their turn

Economic Argument

Fairness across time requires records—ledgers, tallies, or common knowledge. Periodic settling slashes transaction costs compared to constant barter.

Key Features

  • Known money supply = distributed ledger
  • Society's collective memory of fairness
  • Counterfeiting despised (falsifies records)
  • Periodic settlement reduces costs

Historical Example

Tally sticks in medieval England
Split wooden sticks recorded debts. Both parties kept half, ensuring accurate records and preventing fraud.
